The step of the stepper motor is equal to 1.8deg(see table). A reduction ratio of 1:18 was set on as the ratio for this product. It will give us a big range of incline values of the platform from which we can choose and all that will be needed to reprogram the platform to step with another number of steps is to change the value of constants that are all placed at the beginning of code.

Most stepper motor drivers allow the reduction ratio of up to 1:16. For our stepper motor driver, namely, DRV8825, a value of 1:2 was used. From the tests it is obvious that such reduction ratio is the best tradeoff between reduction ratio and the amount of deviation from expected shaft position (see graph in hackaday reference).

That leaves a reduction ratio of 1:9 for the planetary gearbox to accomplish. Since planetary gearboxes with high one-stage reduction ratio are hard to make, a two-stage design was preferred.

Each stage has to have a reduction ratio of 1:3.

Mathematically, planetary gearbox has to satisfy the following equations to be physically realisable (insert https://www.tec-science.com/mechanical-power-transmission/planetary-gear/fundamental-equation-of-planetary-gears-willis-equation/#mjx-eqn-pln):
1.)Z_ring = Z_sun + 2*Z_planet, where they represent the number of teeth on the ring, sun and planet gears respectively -- this is just the requirement for the sun and ring gears to be able to fit inside the ring gear
2.)(Z_ring+Z_s)/(N_planets) = k, k є N ( condition for the planets to be equally spaced, otherwise the forces from the sun gears acting on the planet gear don't cancel out, making the sun gear wobble in a circular fashion as the sun gears rotate around it, shiftinig the net force vector around)

The transmission ratio of a carrier which supports planet gears when the ring gear is stationary, sun gear is driving is defined with this formula(https://www.tec-science.com/mechanical-power-transmission/planetary-gear/transmission-ratios-of-planetary-gears-willis-equation/#mjx-eqn-pl ; Fixed ring gear section):
i_s = 1+z_r/z_s,
where i_s the transmission ratio, z_r and z_s denote the number of teeth on the ring and sun gears respectively. Taking into acount the above equations, a following teeth count for each gear was chosen:
z_p = 16, z_s = 32, z_r = 64.

The gear sizes were recalculated using the formula d = m*z (explain pitch diameter and module)
